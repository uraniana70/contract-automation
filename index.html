01 — Context

A production house.
A crew of 80+.
A paper-based nightmare.

Every film production generates a wave of employment contracts — one per crew member, each with different roles, CCNL levels, periods, and legal clauses. At Play Entertainment SRL, this process was entirely manual: data copied from emails, spreadsheets, and WhatsApp messages into Word documents, one by one.

The production team was spending upward of 25 administrative hours per production on contract compilation alone — before a single frame was shot.

The real bottleneck wasn't the contracts. It was the absence of a single, structured source of truth for crew data.

Key Finding — Discovery Phase
02 — Problem

Four compounding failure points

The manual process created four interconnected problems that worsened with production scale.

01
Data Errors

Tax codes, dates, and CCNL levels entered incorrectly — often discovered only after signing.

02
Time Cost

30 min per contract × 80 crew = 25+ hours of administrative work every production.

03
Duplicate Work

The same data entered multiple times — in the contract, in payroll, in the crew sheet.

04
No Standard

Different contract versions in circulation. No single template. No version control.

03 — Solution

A three-layer pipeline:
data → template → automation

The solution wasn't primarily about AI — it was about designing the right structure around the data. The automation could only work once the data was clean, standardised, and held in a single source of truth.

01
Troupe Data Sheet — Excel

Structured workbook (Specchietto UFB) as single source of truth. Three sheets: Anagrafica, Turni, Template.

[ XLS ]
02
Standardised Contract Template

Master .docx built to CCNL ANICA 07.12.1999. 18 dynamic placeholder fields mapped from the source data.

[ DOC ]
03
Automation Engine — Node.js

Script reads dataset, merges with template, outputs one named contract per crew member in seconds.

[ .JS ]
04
Batch Contract Generation

80+ contracts generated in under 5 seconds. Auto-named files: Contratto_COGNOME_NOME.docx

[ OUT ]
